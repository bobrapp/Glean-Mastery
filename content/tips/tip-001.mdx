## Why this helps

Most weak searches fail before a single keyword is typed: the searcher hasn't
decided what kind of answer they want. Naming the intent first turns a vague
query into a precise one.

## Steps

1. Decide which of three things you want: a **fact**, a **document**, or a
   **person**. Each points you at different keywords.
2. Write the one sentence you'd want the top result to say. That sentence is
   your relevance test.
3. Pick keywords from that sentence — nouns and proper names beat filler words.
4. Run the search and compare the top three results against your test sentence.
5. If they miss, adjust the *intent* (fact vs. document vs. person) before adding
   more words — a longer query rarely fixes a wrong intent.

## Check

Could a colleague tell, from your query alone, whether you wanted a fact, a
document, or a person? If not, sharpen the intent and try again.
