# Day 6 Lab
1. Ask the Assistant a question that matters and read its answer.
2. Open every citation it offers, one at a time.
3. For each, confirm the source actually states what the answer claims.
4. Check the dates — a citation can be real but out of date.
5. If a claim has no citation, or the source doesn't match, treat the answer as unverified.

## Working Example: Verifying a Citation

A cited answer is a starting point, not a verdict. Open each source and confirm it
supports the specific claim before you act on it. Verified against
`assistant_skills`.

```text
Answer claim:  "Travel must be booked 14 days in advance." [1]
[1] Travel Policy (Confluence)

Check 1 — Open [1]. Does it say 14 days, or a different number?
Check 2 — Read the date. Is this the current policy or a 2024 draft?
Check 3 — No citation on a claim? Treat that line as unsupported.

The answer is only as good as the sources you can confirm behind it.
```
