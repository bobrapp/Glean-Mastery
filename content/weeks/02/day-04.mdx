# Day 4 Lab
1. Think of a document you opened recently but can't relocate by title.
2. Search `my:history` alone to see your recent trail of viewed items.
3. Add a keyword — `my:history pricing` — to narrow the trail to that topic.
4. Open the right result and confirm it's the one you remembered.
5. Note how this beats a cold re-search when you have already seen the page.

## Working Example: my:history in Action

The `my:history` filter restricts results to documents you have already viewed, so
re-finding is faster than searching the whole index again. Verified against
`search_filters`.

```text
my:history                  → your recent trail of opened documents
my:history pricing          → only the pricing docs you've already seen
my:history in:"Project Atlas"   → your trail, scoped to one container

Use my:history when you KNOW you saw it — let your own path do the narrowing.
```
