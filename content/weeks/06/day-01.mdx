# Day 1 Lab

**Status: Beta (Glean Fall '25 launch) — confirm GA availability in your tenant before relying on this.**

1. Pick one small, repeatable chore you do by hand — "summarize new #incidents posts each morning," say.
2. In the agent builder, describe that goal in one or two plain sentences. Don't configure anything yet.
3. Read the draft the builder proposes back: its named steps and the apps it wants to touch.
4. Compare the draft to what you actually meant, and note the first place it diverges.
5. Reply in chat to correct that one divergence — then watch the plan redraw.

## Working Example: From a Sentence to a Draft Plan

Per Glean's Fall '25 launch announcement, you build an agent conversationally: you
state the goal, the builder drafts a plan, and you read it before committing.
Verified against `fall25_launch`.

```text
You:    "Each morning, summarize new posts in #incidents and DM me the digest."

Builder draft (proposed, not yet running):
  Step 1  Read new messages in #incidents since last run
  Step 2  Summarize them into a short digest
  Step 3  Send the digest to you via DM

You read the draft and ask: which channel? whose DM? what counts as "new"?
The plan is a starting point you steer — not a finished agent you trust on sight.
```

The vibe-code flow moves the work from filling out forms to reading and correcting a
draft. That's faster, but it means the first real skill is *reviewing the plan* — the
builder's guess about your intent is exactly where MAP-style scrutiny belongs. Treat
this as an announced capability, not a settled one: confirm the builder exists and
behaves this way in your own tenant before you lean on it.
