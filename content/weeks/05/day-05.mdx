# Day 5 Lab
1. Choose a Personal Skill that's proven useful and stable.
2. Review its instructions for anything personal, hard-coded, or stale.
3. Assign an owner who will maintain it after it's shared.
4. Promote it from Personal to Shared scope for your team.
5. Have a teammate run it and confirm it behaves the same for them.

## Working Example: Personal → Shared, With a Review Gate

A Personal Skill is creator-only; a Shared Skill is available to your team.
Promotion is a governance step, not just a toggle — review the instructions and
name an owner before it goes wide. Verified against `assistant_skills`.

```text
Before:  Skill "Weekly status digest"   scope: Personal (only @you)

Review gate (do this first):
  □ Instructions free of personal assumptions ("my Jira" → "the requester's Jira")
  □ No hard-coded names or one-off paths
  □ Owner assigned: @team-lead  (who maintains it going forward)
  □ Description still accurate for a team audience

After:   Skill "Weekly status digest"   scope: Shared (team: Platform)
```

Sharing widens both the benefit and the blast radius: everyone now relies on this
Skill, so a vague instruction or a stale assumption affects the whole team. The
review and a named owner are how you keep a Shared Skill trustworthy over time.
